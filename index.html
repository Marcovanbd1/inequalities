The moment we keep missing

Most support pathways begin after someone has already decided what the problem is, how serious it is, and who to contact.

But many people do not arrive with that clarity. They arrive with uncertainty, stress, and low confidence, or they do not arrive at all.

In that moment, digital exclusion and low digital confidence quietly become clinical risk.

Who this is for

The Local Social is designed for the people most likely to drop off before they reach the right help, including:

  • people without smartphones
  • people with low digital confidence, even if they do have a phone
  • families under pressure, including parents who are exhausted and time-poor
  • young people who do not want to be “handled”, or who fear judgement
  • people carrying long-term stress, trauma, or isolation
  • anyone who finds systems hard to navigate when life is messy

What it is

The Local Social is a simple access layer to trusted support routes, shaped around how people actually behave in the real world.

It offers a small set of calm next steps, in plain language, with local routes first and clear escalation where needed.

It is designed to make finding support easier than staying confused.

It is not “smartphone or nothing”

QR codes are one way in, for people who can use them. But the design principle is wider than QR.

The same routes can travel as a short link sent by text, read out over the phone, shared in conversation, or printed as simple one-page options for people who need paper.

The point is not the technology. The point is the access.

One neighbourhood, many doorways.

Why it matters for inequalities

The people most likely to struggle with navigation are often the people already carrying the most.

When the next step is unclear, or feels embarrassing, or requires confidence people do not have, they delay, minimise, or disappear. Then needs escalate and the system meets them later, louder, and more expensively.

This is an upstream intervention, designed to reduce inequality before it compounds.

Less is more

The Local Social is intentionally small. It does not try to be a comprehensive directory. It focuses on a few high-value routes that matter in moments of pressure.

It is designed to feel human, quick, and safe, especially for people who are tired, stressed, or uncertain.

The promise

We make finding support easier than staying confused.

Boundaries: The Local Social is not medical advice and it is not a replacement for professional judgement. It is a simple access layer to trusted support routes, designed to reduce friction, reduce shame, and reduce drop-off.
